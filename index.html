NeuroMotus: Brain-Computer Interface for Cerebral Palsy

Independent Research Project | 2020 ‚Äì 2024

Problem: Current exoskeletons for Cerebral Palsy patients are limited to basic walking patterns, leaving patients unable to perform essential daily movements like climbing stairs, sitting down, or standing up.

Solution: Developed NeuroMotus, a novel brain-computer interface that predicts user movement intent before physical motion begins. The system uses EEG signals to detect Bereitschaftspotential (readiness potential) occurring 1-2 seconds before voluntary movement.

Technical Implementation: Built a custom CNN architecture with 4 convolutional layers, batch normalization, and dropout regularization. The system processes 5-channel EEG data from motor cortex electrodes (CZ, C1, C2, C3, C4) positioned using the international 10-20 system.

Results: Achieved 94% accuracy in binary movement classification and 87% accuracy for 6-class movement prediction (stand up, sit down, step up, step down, walk forward, rest). Successfully expanded exoskeleton capabilities beyond traditional walking assistance.

Impact: This breakthrough enables CP patients to use exoskeletons in real-world settings, dramatically improving mobility and independence for daily activities.
